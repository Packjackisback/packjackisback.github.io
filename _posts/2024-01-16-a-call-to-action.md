My school district has decided it would be a good idea to ban students' personal devices, ignoring the obvious negative repercussions. 
  For one, extracurriculars are near impossible now, as without phones coordinating pickups during the school day is only possible through email, which many guardians either don't have, or don't check. For a district that seems to pride itself on extracurriculars, this is very troubling. Aditionally, this ban limits student safety, as if you run into trouble on the way to or from school, you will have no way of contacting the authorities. 
  
  I think this really reflects the intention of the board members, who were elected onto the board solely because of the [Republican takeover](https://www.npr.org/2023/01/10/1148211343/a-coordinated-nationwide-agenda-dominated-local-school-board-elections-last-year) of our school board. Because of their lack of interest in improving our school district, they have forced their antiquated ideals on the students, deciding that the inefficiency of today's school system is caused by our personal devices, when the root of the problem lies in the outdated teaching methods. 

 Students won't have the option to opt out, as indicated on the [FAQ page](https://www.katyisd.org/Page/10542), and forced to use outdated, slow Chromebooks. This will especially impact computer science students, or most of the student body, as these Chromebooks will be unable to execute C++ or Java code. 
  ![image](https://github.com/Packjackisback/packjackisback.github.io/assets/83289186/5cbcb990-6883-4f02-89a7-8f4548eefac8)

  I really object to this policy, as this furthers Google's attempts to collect as much data as possible. We don't consent to any of this, the Google suite and Google controlled devices are thrust upon us, with accounts created with our name, date of birth, and much more. I don't want Google having this kind of information on me, and I especially don't want a working microphone and camera connected to Google's servers in my private house. 
   Google provides affordable, simple solutions for schools for almost free, but I know their motives aren't pure. By getting users while they are young, Google ensures that most children around the country are familiar with the Google Suite, and will choose it when they grow up. All of the unlimited Drive space, and free Google Classroom integration is all just a scheme to ensure their profitability for another generation.

   What can we do about it? It seems calling against this is just blowing against the wind. What can us students do against an out of touch board?
