My school district has decided it would be a good idea to ban students' personal devices, ignoring the obvious *very* negative reprocussions. 
  For one, extra-curriculars are near impossible now, as cordinating pickups during the school day is only possible through email addresses, which many guardians either don't have, or don't check. For a district that seems to pride itself on only the extra-curriculars, this is very troubling. This ban also really limits student safety, as if you run into trouble on the way to or from school, you will have no way of contacting the authorities. 
  
  I think this really reflects the intention of the board members, who were elected onto the board solely because of the [Republican takeover](https://www.npr.org/2023/01/10/1148211343/a-coordinated-nationwide-agenda-dominated-local-school-board-elections-last-year) of our school board. These board members have clearly only come to power because of the lies told by the [Republican party](https://nymag.com/intelligencer/article/desantis-florida-trump-education-politics.html). Because of their lack of interest in improving our school district, they have forced their antiquated ideals on the students, deciding that the inefficiency of today's school system is caused by personal devices when the root of the problem lies in the outdated teaching methods. 

  Students will be unable to opt out, as per the [FAQ page](https://www.katyisd.org/Page/10542), and forced to use outdated, slow ChromeBooks. This will especially impact CS students, or most of the student body, as these chromebooks will be unable to execute C++ or Java code.
